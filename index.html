01 — About

A Journey From
Telecoms to Classrooms.

I am Diana Adhiambo — a Nairobi-born data professional currently pursuing a Master's degree in Data Science & Analytics at Strathmore University. My path here was not linear, and I think that is exactly what makes my perspective valuable.

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Telecommunication. It was during my attachment at World Vision Kenya in 2018 that everything shifted. I was placed within an Area Development Project as IT Support, and part of my work touched the Learning Toolkit Programme — a technology-integrated learning initiative for children. What I witnessed in those lab sessions changed me. The children were electric with excitement. Teachers who had never used these devices before were leaning in, curious and engaged. It was the first time I saw, with my own eyes, what happens when access meets opportunity in a classroom.

That conviction deepened when I later worked at the Save the Children East and Southern Africa Regional Office, tracking how technology was being implemented across the region — and realising the missing piece was data that could guide decisions. That question is what brought me to data science. I am compassionate, zealous, and ambitious — and I bring all three into every dataset I work with.
