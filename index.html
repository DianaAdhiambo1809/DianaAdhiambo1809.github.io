01 — About

Driven by Data,
Guided by Purpose

I am a first-year Data Science & Analytics student at Strathmore University in Nairobi, Kenya, with a focused interest in applying data to solve some of the world's most pressing challenges — particularly in education access and quality for children in Sub-Saharan Africa.

My work sits at the intersection of technical data skills and humanitarian sector knowledge. I am building expertise in the tools NGOs and UN agencies actually use: SQL for data management, Power BI for stakeholder dashboards, Python for predictive modelling, and GIS for geographic analysis of educational gaps.

I am particularly passionate about Education in Emergencies (EiE) — understanding how conflict, displacement, and poverty interrupt children's education, and how data can help organizations like UNICEF, IRC, and Save the Children respond more effectively. I anchor all my work to the INEE Minimum Standards and SDG 4 indicators.

Based in Nairobi — the operational hub of East African humanitarian response — I am actively building a portfolio that speaks directly to the needs of the sector.

🎓 KENYA — 47 COUNTIES

Kenya Education Gap Analysis — Interactive Dashboard

A county-level interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing educational inequality across Kenya's 47 counties. Integrates 15 variables including out-of-school children counts, gender parity index, pupil-teacher ratios, school infrastructure scores, and distance-to-school barriers. Built to support NGO program officers in identifying where interventions are needed most.

Student Dropout Risk Prediction Model

A binary classification model predicting which primary school children are at highest risk of dropping out before completing school. Uses household poverty, attendance rate, distance, and gender as key features. Outputs risk scores integrated into a Power BI map for program targeting.
