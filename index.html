01 — About

A Journey From
Telecoms to Classrooms.

I am Diana Adhiambo — a Nairobi-born data professional currently pursuing a Master's degree in Data Science & Analytics at Strathmore University. My path here was not linear, and I think that is exactly what makes my perspective valuable.

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Telecommunication. It was during my attachment at World Vision Kenya in 2018 that everything shifted. I was placed within an Area Development Project as IT Support, and part of my work touched the Learning Toolkit Programme — a technology-integrated learning initiative for children. What I witnessed in those lab sessions changed me. The children were electric with excitement. Teachers who had never used these devices before were leaning in, curious and engaged. It was the first time I saw, with my own eyes, what happens when access meets opportunity in a classroom. I knew then that this intersection of technology and education was where I wanted to contribute.

That conviction deepened when I later worked at the Save the Children East and Southern Africa Regional Office, supporting technology for development initiatives. I had the opportunity to engage with education programmes across multiple countries in the region — tracking how technology was being implemented, what was working, and where the gaps remained. It became clear to me that the missing piece was not always resources or goodwill — it was data that could guide decisions. Which children were being left behind? Where were the gender disparities? Which counties needed teachers most urgently?

That question is what brought me to data science. I believe that data, when used with compassion and purpose, can be the bridge between a child's potential and their reality. My goal is to work within the humanitarian and NGO sector — using analytics, predictive modelling, and data visualisation to ensure that education decisions are not made blindly, but are backed by evidence that puts children at the centre.

I am compassionate, zealous, and ambitious — and I bring all three into every dataset I work with.
